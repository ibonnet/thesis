Venezuela's aviation sector has emerged from a decade of isolation.

Air traffic has resumed across the country.

Two decades ago, the Miami-Caracas route was one of the most popular international flight destinations in the U.S. with over 1.2 million passenger seats sold per year and 40 daily flights connecting the two countries.

Today, there are currently no direct flights between the U.S. and Venezuela.

The country's aviation sector has been severely impacted over the past decade, marked by economic and political instability, as well as safety concerns. This quickly led to a complete isolation from the international community. In 2019, the United States suspended all air transportation to Venezuela, further exacerbating the country’s isolation.
